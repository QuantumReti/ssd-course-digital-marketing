# Module 01: Local SEO Fundamentals

## What Is Local SEO?

Local SEO is the practice of optimising a business's online presence so it appears prominently when people nearby search for the services it offers. For a plumber in Chermside, local SEO means when someone searches plumber Chermside or plumber near me, their business appears at the top of Google.

Local SEO is the single highest-ROI digital marketing activity for service businesses. Unlike paid ads, organic rankings are essentially free traffic once earned - and they build over time.

---

## How Google Decides Who Ranks Locally

Google uses three core factors to determine local search rankings:

### 1. Proximity
How physically close is the business to the person searching? Google uses the searcher's location and compares it to the business's registered address. Ensure your address is correctly registered everywhere.

### 2. Relevance
How well does the business match what the searcher is looking for? Use the exact words people search for in your business description, services list, and website content.

### 3. Prominence
How well-known and trusted is the business online? Prominence is built through:
- Number and quality of Google reviews
- Consistency of business information across the web (citations)
- Quality and authority of your website
- Links from other websites
- Social media presence and activity

Key insight: A newer business with 80 Google reviews and an optimised profile will often outrank an established business with 8 reviews and a neglected profile.

## Google Business Profile Optimisation

The Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important local SEO asset. It is the card that appears in Google Maps and the local pack (the 3 businesses shown at the top of search results).

### Business Category
Choose the most specific primary category possible. This is the number 1 ranking factor most businesses get wrong.
- Wrong: Contractor
- Right: Plumber (exact category)
- Pro: Set 2-3 additional categories for related services (e.g., Drainage Service, Hot Water System Supplier)

### Business Description
Write a 150-200 word description that:
- Uses the top 3-5 keywords your customers search for
- Mentions the suburbs you serve
- Describes your core services in plain language
- Includes a soft call to action (Call us today for a free quote)

Example for a plumber:
Brisbane Plumbing and Gas is a family-owned plumbing company serving the northern suburbs of Brisbane including Chermside, Aspley, Bridgeman Downs, and Albany Creek. We specialise in emergency plumbing, hot water systems, gas fitting, and general plumbing repairs. With over 15 years in the industry and 120+ Google reviews, we are trusted by hundreds of Brisbane households and businesses. Available 24/7 for emergencies - call us today for a fast, honest quote.

### Photos
Google prioritises businesses with high-quality, regularly updated photos.

Required photos:
- Logo (clear, high resolution)
- Cover photo (team, van, or storefront)
- Interior or exterior photos
- Work photos (before and after, completed jobs)
- Team photos

Minimum: 10 photos. Target: 20+ photos.
Update frequency: Add 2-3 new photos per month.

### Google Posts
Use Google Posts to share updates, offers, and news directly on your listing. Posts signal to Google that the business is active.

Post types: What's New (general updates), Offer (promotions), Event (workshops, open days)

Posting frequency: Minimum 1 post per week. Target 2x per week.

### Reviews - The Most Important Ranking Factor

A business with 100 reviews at 4.8 stars will get significantly more clicks than one with 10 reviews at 5.0 stars.

The review ask (exact script):
Hey [Name], thanks so much for using us - really appreciate your business. Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It takes about 30 seconds and it really helps us out. Here is the direct link: [Google review link]

Review strategy:
- Ask every happy customer within 24 hours of job completion
- Send via WhatsApp (highest response rate)
- Follow up once if no response after 3 days
- Never offer incentives for reviews (Google policy violation)

Responding to reviews:
- Respond to EVERY review, positive or negative, within 48 hours
- Positive: Thank them + personalise + invite return visit
- Negative: Acknowledge + apologise + offer to resolve offline
- Google rewards review responses with ranking boosts

### Q and A Section
Populate the Q and A section yourself with the top 5-10 questions customers ask.

Example Q and As for a plumber:
- Q: Do you offer emergency plumbing? A: Yes - we are available 24/7 for emergencies across Brisbane's northern suburbs.
- Q: Do you provide free quotes? A: Yes, we provide free no-obligation quotes for all plumbing work.

## On-Page SEO for Service Businesses

Your website must signal to Google exactly what you do and where you do it.

### Title Tags
Formula: [Primary Service] in [City/Suburb] | [Business Name]
Example: Plumber in Chermside | Brisbane Plumbing and Gas

### Meta Descriptions
Formula: [What you do] + [location served] + [key benefit] + [CTA]
Example: Expert plumbing services in Chermside and surrounding suburbs. Fast response, upfront pricing, and over 120 five-star reviews. Call for a free quote today.

### H1 Heading
Every page should have exactly ONE H1 heading that includes the primary keyword.
Example: Professional Plumbing Services in Brisbane's North

### H2 Headings
Use H2 headings to structure service pages and include secondary keywords.
Examples:
- Emergency Plumbing in Chermside
- Hot Water System Installation and Repair
- Blocked Drain Specialists - Northern Suburbs

### Local Keywords
Throughout page content, naturally weave in:
- Primary suburb + service (plumber in Aspley)
- Surrounding suburbs (plumbing services Bridgeman Downs, Albany Creek, Carseldine)
- Near me equivalent language (plumber close to you in Brisbane's north)

## NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone Number. Google cross-references your NAP across dozens of websites and directories. Inconsistencies confuse Google and hurt rankings.

The rule: Your NAP must be IDENTICAL everywhere it appears online.

Common inconsistency culprits:
- St vs Street in addresses
- Different phone number formats
- Old business name still listed somewhere
- Old address after a move

Audit process: Google your business name and check every listing. Correct any inconsistencies manually.

## Local Citation Building

Citations are online mentions of your business NAP - even without a link. They signal to Google that your business is legitimate and established.

Tier 1 (Must Have):
- Google Business Profile
- Apple Maps
- Bing Places
- Facebook Business Page
- Yellow Pages Australia (yellowpages.com.au)
- True Local (truelocal.com.au)

Tier 2 (Should Have):
- Hotfrog (hotfrog.com.au)
- Yelp Australia
- StartLocal
- Local.com.au
- hipages (for tradies)
- Oneflare (for tradies)

Industry-Specific:
- AHPRA register (health businesses)
- Law Society directory (lawyers)
- Master Builders Queensland (builders)
- NECA (electricians)

## Tracking Local SEO Success

### Google Business Insights
Key metrics:
- Searches: How many times your profile appeared in search
- Views: How many people clicked on your profile
- Direction requests: People navigating to you
- Website clicks: Traffic sent to your site
- Phone calls: Direct calls from your listing

Benchmark: Established businesses in competitive suburbs should see 500-2,000+ monthly searches.

### Google Search Console
Key metrics:
- Total clicks (from organic search)
- Total impressions (times your site appeared in results)
- Average click-through rate (aim for 5%+ for branded, 2-3% for non-branded)
- Average position (aim for top 10 for primary keywords)

### Rank Tracking
Track monthly for each client:
- [service] [primary suburb]
- [service] [secondary suburb]
- [service] Brisbane
- [service] near me (check from their suburb location)
