# Module 02: Google Ads for SMBs

## When Does Google Ads Make Sense?

Use Google Ads when:
- The business needs leads immediately (new business, seasonal rush)
- Organic ranking will take 3-6 months and they cannot wait
- They have a specific promotion with a limited window
- Their SEO is established but they want to dominate top commercial keywords
- High-value, low-volume service (dental implants at 5K a job, worth a 50 CPC)

Use SEO (Organic) instead when:
- Budget is under 500 per month (not enough to compete effectively)
- They want long-term, sustainable traffic without ongoing ad spend
- The industry is not overly competitive (organic rankings achievable in 3-4 months)
- They have burned money on ads with poor results (trust rebuild needed first)

Use Both when:
- Budget allows (1,500+ per month total)
- Short-term (ads) and long-term (organic) goals both exist
- Highly competitive market where ads are needed to even appear

## Search vs Display vs Performance Max

### Search Ads
Text-based ads that appear at the top of Google search results when someone searches specific keywords.

Best for: Service businesses with clear, high-intent search terms (emergency plumber Brisbane, dentist Chermside, wedding photographer Brisbane).

Why it works: Captures people at the exact moment they are looking to buy. Highest commercial intent of any ad format.

When to use for Brisbane SMBs: Always start here. It is the most predictable and measurable format.

### Display Ads
Image-based ads shown across millions of websites in the Google Display Network.

Best for: Brand awareness, remarketing (showing ads to people who already visited the site).

Not recommended for: Pure service businesses trying to generate leads - too many impressions, too few conversions.

For Brisbane SMBs: Skip for lead generation. Use only for remarketing if budget allows.

### Performance Max (PMax)
Google's AI-driven campaign type that automatically shows ads across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, and Maps.

Best for: Businesses with clear conversion goals and enough data for Google's AI to learn (minimum 30-50 conversions per month).

Warning for SMBs: PMax can look impressive on a dashboard (high impressions, lots of activity) while delivering poor-quality leads. Stick to Search campaigns until you have solid data.

## Keyword Research for Local Service Businesses

### Keyword Match Types

Exact Match [keyword]: Ad shows only when someone searches that exact term or close variants.
Example: [plumber brisbane northside] - shows for plumber brisbane northside or plumbers brisbane northside.
Use for: High-value, high-intent terms you want to control precisely.

Phrase Match "keyword": Ad shows when the search contains the phrase in order plus other words.
Example: "plumber brisbane" - shows for emergency plumber brisbane, plumber brisbane northside, best plumber brisbane.
Use for: Capturing variations while maintaining some control.

Broad Match keyword: Ad shows for searches related to the keyword - Google decides.
Warning: Broad match on a small budget = wasted spend. Avoid unless running large campaigns with strong negative keyword lists.

### Building a Keyword List for a Brisbane Tradie

Core service + location:
- plumber brisbane
- plumber northside brisbane
- plumber [specific suburb]
- emergency plumber brisbane

Service-specific:
- blocked drain brisbane
- hot water system brisbane
- gas plumber brisbane

Intent-based:
- plumber near me
- plumber open now
- 24 hour plumber brisbane

### Negative Keywords - Critical for SMBs

Negative keywords prevent your ad from showing for irrelevant searches.

Universal negatives for service businesses:
- jobs, careers, apprenticeship (someone looking for employment)
- diy, how to (not looking to hire)
- free (price-sensitive, unlikely to convert)
- course, training, certification (industry education, not a customer)
- competitor names (unless running specific competitor campaigns)

## Writing Ads That Get Clicks

Google allows 3 headlines (30 chars each) and 2 descriptions (90 chars each) for responsive search ads.

### Headline Formula
Headline 1: Service + Location (relevance) - Emergency Plumber Brisbane
Headline 2: Key Benefit or USP (reason to click) - 24/7 Response - 60 Min Arrival
Headline 3: Trust Signal or CTA - 120+ Five-Star Reviews. Call Now

### Description Formula
Description 1: Expand on the benefit + specific detail
Blocked drains, hot water systems and gas fitting. Fast, reliable plumbers serving Brisbane's north. Upfront pricing guaranteed.

Description 2: Address objection + CTA
No call-out fee for quotes. Licensed and insured. Same-day service available. Call now for a free assessment.

### Trust Signals That Boost CTR
- Licensed and Insured
- 5-Star Rated or 4.9 Stars on Google
- 20+ Years Experience
- Brisbane Local
- Same Day Service
- No Hidden Fees

### Ad Extensions (Now Called Assets - Use All of Them)
- Call Extension: Shows phone number directly in ad
- Location Extension: Shows address and distance
- Sitelink Extensions: Additional links (Emergency Service, Get a Quote, Our Reviews)
- Callout Extensions: Short snippets (Free Quotes, 24/7 Available, No Lock-In Contracts)

## Landing Page Requirements

Clicks mean nothing without conversions. A poorly designed landing page kills your return on ad spend.

### The Perfect Service Business Landing Page

1. Specific to the ad: If the ad says Emergency Plumber Brisbane, the landing page must be about emergency plumbing. Sending all traffic to the homepage = wasted clicks.

2. Fast: Page must load in under 3 seconds on mobile. Use Google's PageSpeed Insights to test. Slow pages = higher bounce rates = lower Quality Score = higher cost per click.

3. Mobile-First: 60-70% of local service searches are on mobile. Large phone number button at the top. Easy-to-fill form.

4. One Clear CTA: Every landing page needs ONE primary action:
   - Call Now (phone button)
   - Get a Free Quote (form)
   - Book Online (booking calendar)

5. Trust Elements Above the Fold: Before they scroll, they should see:
   - Your business name and what you do
   - A real phone number
   - Star rating and review count
   - Suburb or serving Brisbane statement

## Budget Guidance for Brisbane SMBs

Under 500 per month:
- Difficult to compete for high-value keywords
- Not enough data for Google to optimise
- Consider starting with SEO instead

500-1,000 per month (Starter):
- Focus on 3-5 high-intent, long-tail keywords
- Single campaign, one ad group per service
- Works well for niche services with limited competition

1,000-1,500 per month (Growth):
- Multiple campaigns (different services)
- Can compete for core keywords in most Brisbane suburbs
- Enough data to optimise within 4-6 weeks

1,500+ per month (Competitive):
- Compete for top-volume, high-competition keywords
- Remarketing possible
- Can dominate specific suburbs

## Quality Score: What It Is and Why It Matters

Quality Score (1-10) is Google's rating of the relevance and quality of your ad, keyword, and landing page. A higher Quality Score = lower cost per click for the same position.

Components:
1. Expected click-through rate (historical CTR for the keyword)
2. Ad relevance (how well the ad matches the keyword)
3. Landing page experience (how useful and relevant the landing page is)

Why it matters: A business with Quality Score 8 pays roughly half as much per click as one with Quality Score 4 for the same ad position.

How to improve:
- Ensure keywords match ad text (if bidding on emergency plumber, use emergency plumber in the headline)
- Improve landing page relevance and load speed
- Improve ad CTR by testing better headlines

## Conversion Tracking Setup

Without conversion tracking, you are flying blind.

Essential Conversions to Track:

Phone Calls:
- Google Ads call extension clicks (mobile)
- Phone number on landing page (via Google forwarding number)
- Goal: Call duration over 60 seconds = qualified call

Form Fills:
- Thank-you page view after form submission
- Or: Form submission event via Google Tag Manager

Direct Bookings:
- Booking confirmation page view
- Or: Booking system event (if integrated with GA4)

## When to Pause or Kill an Ad

Pause the ad when:
- 100+ impressions and 0 clicks: Bad headline or wrong keyword
- Cost per conversion over 3x target: Something broken in the funnel

Fix the landing page when:
- 20+ clicks and 0 conversions: People are clicking but not acting
- Bounce rate over 70% on ad landing page: Page does not match the ad

Kill the campaign (or restructure) when:
- 3 months of consistent data and cost per lead is unsustainable
- Click patterns show significant irrelevant traffic despite negative keywords
- Client has run out of capacity to handle leads (great problem - pause and restart)
