# Module 05: Email Marketing

## Why Email Still Works

Email marketing has a higher ROI than any other digital marketing channel - consistently around 36-42 return for every 1 dollar spent. For small businesses, it is the most direct way to stay in front of existing customers and nurture prospects.

Unlike social media, you own your email list. Algorithm changes do not affect it. You are not renting an audience - you are building an asset.

---

## Building an Email List from a Service Business

Sources for building the list:

Existing customers: Every person who has ever done business with you. Their email should be in your CRM, invoicing system, or phone contacts.

New enquiries: Everyone who fills in your website contact form or books online provides an email. Auto-add to the list.

WhatsApp leads: Ask for email at the close. What email should I send the confirmation to? Now it is in the list.

In-person: At point of service, Would you mind leaving your email? We send monthly tips and the occasional special offer - never spam.

### The Legal Requirement (Australia)
Under the Australian Spam Act, you need consent to send commercial emails. Consent can be:
- Expressed: They ticked a box or explicitly said yes
- Inferred: They gave you their email in a commercial context (e.g., buying something)

Always include an unsubscribe link in every email. This is not optional - it is required by law.

## Welcome Sequence: The 3-Email Series

Every new subscriber should receive a welcome sequence automatically.

### Email 1: Welcome + Context
Send: Immediately upon subscribing
Subject line: Welcome to [Business Name] - here is what to expect

Content:
- Warm intro: who you are, what you do
- What they will receive (tips, updates, offers - not spam)
- One genuine piece of value right now (a tip, a resource, a checklist)
- Soft CTA: Follow on social, check out a blog post

Example opener:
Hey [First Name]! I am Josh from Sun State Digital. You signed up because you are probably looking to get your business found online - and I am really glad you did.

Over the next few weeks I will share some of the most practical tips I know for getting more clients from Google. No fluff, no daily emails - just useful stuff when it matters.

To kick things off, here is the number 1 thing most Brisbane businesses get wrong on Google (and how to fix it in 10 minutes)...

### Email 2: Deliver Value
Send: 2-3 days after Email 1
Subject line: [Service tip relevant to their industry/opt-in]

Content:
- One substantial value piece (how-to guide, checklist, insight)
- No selling. Pure value.
- CTA: Hit reply if you have any questions (drives replies = builds deliverability)

### Email 3: The Offer
Send: 5-7 days after Email 2
Subject line: Ready to sort your online presence? Here is what we do.

Content:
- Reference the journey: You have been on the list for a week - by now you know we give real, practical advice.
- Present the offer clearly: what it is, what it includes, what it costs
- Social proof: one specific result from a Brisbane client
- Clear CTA: link to book or buy

## Monthly Newsletter Structure

### The 4-Part Newsletter Formula

Part 1: Local Hook (2-3 sentences)
Tie in something happening in Brisbane or relevant to their industry.
With summer in full swing and Queenslanders searching for [services] more than ever, we have been flat out helping businesses make the most of the season...

Part 2: The Main Tip (150-200 words)
One actionable piece of advice. Enough detail to be useful, not so much it overwhelms.

Part 3: What We Have Been Up To (50-100 words)
Brief update: recent client launches, new services, team news.
This month we launched sites for a dentist in Fortitude Valley, a plumbing company in the northern suburbs, and a cafe in West End. Proud of all three - if you want to see them, just reply and ask.

Part 4: The CTA (1-2 lines)
One offer or next step. Never more than one.
If you have been sitting on getting started - now is a good time. We have got 5 spots open this week: [link]

## Reactivation Campaigns

Target subscribers who have not opened or clicked in 90+ days.

### The 3-Email Reactivation Sequence

Email 1 - The Check-In:
Subject: Still there, [First Name]?

Hey [Name] - it has been a while since we have heard from you. Life gets busy, I get it. Just checking in to see if getting online is still on your radar - or if you would prefer I stop sending these (no hard feelings!). Hit reply either way.

Email 2 - The Offer (3 days later):
Subject: Last chance - quick thing for [their business type]

Short, personal-feeling email. One specific offer relevant to them. Clear deadline.

Email 3 - The Goodbye (5 days after Email 2):
Subject: Taking you off the list... unless?

I am going to stop sending emails after this one - I do not want to be in your inbox if it is not adding value. If you would like to stay on the list, just click here. Otherwise, no worries at all - wish you all the best with the business.

Emails like these often get the highest open and reply rates because the subject lines create genuine curiosity and the tone is human.

## Segmentation Basics

### Segment 1: New Leads (Never Bought)
Goal: Build trust, overcome hesitation, present the offer.
Content: Educational + soft sell. Focus on social proof and low-risk entry.

### Segment 2: Active Clients (Currently Paying)
Goal: Retain, upsell, generate referrals.
Content: Tips to get more from current services, sneak peeks at new features, referral offers.

### Segment 3: Past Clients (No Longer Active)
Goal: Win-back + referral.
Content: We have launched some new things since you were with us. Highlight improvements. Special return offer.

### Segment 4: Engaged Non-Buyers (Opening emails but not converting)
Goal: Convert.
Content: Stronger social proof, limited offers, direct ask - What is holding you back?

Formula 4: Local Relevance

## Email Metrics Benchmarks

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| Open Rate | less than 15% | 20-25% | 30-40% | 40%+ |
| Click Rate | less than 1% | 2-3% | 4-6% | 6%+ |
| Unsubscribe Rate | over 1% | 0.3-0.5% | under 0.3% | under 0.1% |
| Reply Rate | under 0.1% | 0.2-0.5% | 1%+ | 2%+ |

A high unsubscribe rate (over 1%) usually means: wrong audience, wrong frequency, or content mismatch.

A low open rate (under 15%) usually means: subject lines need work, or the list has gone cold.
